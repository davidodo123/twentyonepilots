GRAMMYS

Twenty One Pilots is an American musical duo formed by Tyler Joseph and Josh Dun. They became famous for their unique mix of alternative rock, pop, hip-hop, and electronic music. In 2017, they won their first Grammy Award for Best Pop Duo/Group Performance with their hit song "Stressed Out". Their Grammy win became one of the most memorable moments in the show’s history. Before going on stage, Tyler and Josh took off their pants, walking up to receive the award in their underwear.

BIOGRAPHY

Twenty One Pilots is an American musical duo from Columbus, Ohio, formed in 2009 by Tyler Joseph along with former members Nick Thomas and Chris Salih. After releasing their self-titled debut album that same year, the group began building a small but loyal following in their hometown. Their second self-released record, Regional at Best (2011), revealed the band’s developing style, a distinctive mix of alternative rock, pop, hip-hop, and electronic elements combined with deeply personal lyrics. Shortly after that release, Thomas and Salih left the band, and Joseph continued the project with drummer Josh Dun, forming the lineup that would soon bring the duo international attention.

Their lyrics often explore themes of mental health, anxiety, faith, and identity — transforming vulnerability into empowerment. Through introspective songwriting and high-energy performances, Twenty One Pilots has built one of the most loyal fan bases in modern music, known as the Skeleton Clique. The duo gained mainstream recognition with their 2015 album Blurryface, featuring global hits like “Stressed Out” and “Ride.” This success was followed by Trench (2018), a concept-driven album expanding their creative universe through layered storytelling and symbolism. In 2024, the release of Clancy marked the conclusion of a narrative arc that spanned nearly a decade a journey of struggle, growth, and self-acceptance. Today, Twenty One Pilots continues to evolve, fusing visual art, emotional honesty, and sonic experimentation to deliver experiences that are as cinematic as they are personal.

Vessel & Major Label

In 2012, their energetic live performances and unique sound attracted the interest of Fueled by Ramen, a subsidiary of Atlantic Records, leading to their first major-label deal. The following year they released Vessel, which included songs like “Holding on to You,” “Car Radio,” and “House of Gold.” The album explored anxiety, faith, and self-doubt through an energetic and experimental sound that resonated with a growing audience.

Blurryface

With the release of Blurryface, Twenty One Pilots achieved global recognition. The album explored identity, insecurity, and internal conflict, personified through the character “Blurryface.” Hit singles such as “Stressed Out,” “Ride,” and “Tear in My Heart” dominated charts worldwide, propelling the band to arena tours and cementing their reputation for theatrical, emotionally charged performances.

Trench & Expanded Universe

After the success of Blurryface, the duo built an elaborate fictional world within Trench, exploring themes of freedom, rebellion, and mental health. Fans were drawn into the mysterious world of Dema and the Banditos, deepening the narrative universe surrounding the band. Tracks like “Jumpsuit” and “Chlorine” showcased a darker, more cinematic tone.

Clancy & Closure

Released during the pandemic, Scaled and Icy presented a brighter, more optimistic sound — a contrast to their earlier, darker records. The album represented both disconnection and resilience, offering messages of hope in isolation. Songs like “Shy Away” and “Saturday” became instant fan favorites, symbolizing renewal and adaptability.
